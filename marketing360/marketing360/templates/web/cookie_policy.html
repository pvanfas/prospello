Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 15, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how 360 Marketing School ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies on our website, and how you can manage your cookie preferences. By using our website and services, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners. Cookies allow websites to recognize your device and remember your preferences, such as login information, language settings, and other choices you make.

Cookies do not contain any personally identifiable information unless you have provided such information to us. They cannot damage your device or read data from other files on your device.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes to enhance your experience on our website and to provide our services effectively:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features.
  • Analytics Cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.
  • Marketing Cookies: These cookies are used to track visitors across websites to display relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. They help our website remember your actions during a single browsing session.

3.2 Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They help us recognize you when you return to our website and remember your preferences.

3.3 First-Party Cookies: These are cookies set directly by our website on your device.

3.4 Third-Party Cookies: These are cookies set by third-party services that appear on our pages, such as analytics providers, advertising networks, or social media platforms.

4. Specific Cookies We Use

We may use the following types of cookies on our website:

  • Authentication Cookies: To identify you when you log in and maintain your session
  • Preference Cookies: To remember your settings and preferences
  • Security Cookies: To protect against fraud and maintain security
  • Performance Cookies: To analyze how our website is used and improve performance
  • Targeting Cookies: To deliver relevant content and advertisements

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages. These third parties may include:

  • Google Analytics for website usage analytics
  • Social media platforms for social sharing functionality
  • Advertising networks for personalized advertising
  • Payment processors for secure payment handling

We do not control these third-party cookies, and you should check their respective privacy and cookie policies for information about how they use cookies.

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies if you prefer. However, disabling cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our website.

6.1 Browser Settings: You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • See what cookies you have and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from particular sites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

6.2 Opt-Out Tools: You can opt out of certain third-party cookies by visiting their opt-out pages or using industry opt-out tools.

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly:

  • You may not be able to log in to your account
  • Your preferences may not be saved
  • Some website features may not be available
  • The website may not function as efficiently

8. Cookies and Personal Data

Our use of cookies is governed by our Privacy Policy, which explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information. Some cookies may collect personal data, and we process this data in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

9. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our practices. When we make changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies.

10. Your Consent
