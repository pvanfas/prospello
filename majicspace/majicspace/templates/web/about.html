Our Story

At Majicspace Atelier, we don’t just design buildings—we shape emotions, craft silences, and give form to your dreams.

Our journey began in 2018, led by Abdul Majid, with a deep discomfort: watching homes built to impress the world outside, but failing the people within. Spaces designed without listening—to the land, to the light, or to the lives that would unfold inside.

So we chose another way. We design with empathy and intent, creating spaces that are personal, functional, and quietly joyful. For us, architecture is not about loud statements—it’s about serenity, belonging, and beauty that reveals itself over time.

We work closely with each client, not to control, but to co-create. Guiding their vision into reality with honesty, care, and craft. Because we believe a home should not only look beautiful—it should feel like home.

At Majicspace, we don’t follow trends. We follow truth.

And in every project, we search for that quiet magic where architecture meets soul.

Abdul Majid – Founder & Creative Director, MajicSpace

Abdul Majid is the founder and creative head of MajicSpace, a design and development studio that blends architectural imagination with engineering precision. Trained as a civil engineer, Abdul’s entry into the world of architecture wasn’t by chance — it was the result of conscious exploration, grounded mentorship, and years of hands-on learning.

After completing his engineering degree, Abdul began his design journey under the guidance of Ar. Jose Thomas, a prominent name in the architectural community. This early experience was foundational — shaping his design sensibilities, helping him understand space-making beyond textbooks, and giving him direct exposure to the creative and technical depth of real-world architecture.

This mentorship, combined with his engineering background, gave Abdul a rare dual perspective: the creative sensitivity of an architect and the clarity of a builder. Over the years, he has led projects ranging from homes and interiors to large-scale hospitality developments like Banasura View — each crafted with purpose, context, and an eye for beauty.

At MajicSpace, Abdul’s vision is clear: to create spaces that are emotionally resonant, technically sound, and timeless in character. His approach is rooted in deep thinking, storytelling, and a belief that design should move people — not just impress them.
